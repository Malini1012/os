WELCOME TO BANKER'S ALGORITHM

The Banker's Algorithm is a resource allocation and deadlock avoidance algorithm developed by Edsger Dijkstra. It is used in operating systems to allocate resources to processes in a way that avoids deadlocks. The algorithm is called the "Banker's Algorithm" because it simulates the allocation of resources by a banker who only allocates resources if it is safe to do so, similar to how a banker allocates money to clients.
